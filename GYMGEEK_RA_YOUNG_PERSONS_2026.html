Scope

GymGeek runs multiple group sessions weekly. This risk assessment applies to any gym member under the age of 16 attending sessions at GymGeek Weymouth.

— Hazard Register

Potential Hazard Who Is at Risk Existing Control Measures Risk Rating Preventative Measures Required Date Completed
Safeguarding Young Persons Adequate supervision at all times during sessions. Gym may be occupied by other adult customers but there will be no unsupervised contact between adults and young persons. Children's coach present throughout every session. ✓ Low All young persons must have a signed permission slip from their parent/guardian before attending. Shower facilities available but access restricted to one person at a time. All staff with regular contact with under-16s to hold current DBS clearance.
Use of Gym Equipment Young Persons All young persons receive formal training on equipment use and hazards before starting any exercise programme. PAR-Q and health screening form completed and signed. Session booking required in advance. All members must feel well before entering. Medical clearance required for any existing conditions or injuries. Regular floor checks for trip hazards, spills and equipment left out. Adequate lighting throughout. Guardian consent form signed for all under-16s. ✓ Low Misuse of equipment will not be tolerated and may result in exclusion from the session. All equipment fixed in place unless specifically designed to be moved. Young persons must not use equipment outside the scope of their induction.
Service and Maintenance Young Persons Daily equipment checks completed by staff. Any faults or defects reported immediately. Fault logged with equipment supplier — engineer attends within 48 hours. Weekly audit of check sheets. Annual service contracts in place with all equipment suppliers. ✓ Low Out of order signs placed on any affected equipment immediately. Young persons must not use any equipment displaying an out of order sign under any circumstances.
Emergency Evacuation Young Persons Fire evacuation procedure displayed at all exits. Assembly point in car park. Emergency lighting operational. Young persons briefed on evacuation procedure at start of first session. ✓ Low Children's coach responsible for accounting for all young persons during any evacuation. Parent/guardian emergency contact details held on file. Coach to remain with young persons at assembly point until parent/guardian contact is made.
Physical Injury During Exercise Young Persons All sessions supervised by a qualified coach. Load and intensity appropriate to age, fitness level and any disclosed health conditions. Young persons not permitted to use equipment outside their programme without direct supervision. ✓ Low Any injury or complaint of pain to be acted upon immediately — session stopped and first aid administered as required. Incident recorded in the accident log. Parent/guardian contacted immediately in the event of any injury.
